# PPC Guru — Enterprise B2B Readiness Audit

**Lens:** Would a **large B2B enterprise buyer** (CMO / procurement / buying committee evaluating a
6–7-figure retainer) trust this site enough to convert? Three specialist agents reviewed the current
live build (`ec5dfb6`) — trust/credibility, design/UX, and conversion/messaging — then findings were
synthesized into the ranked plan below.

> Scores are 0–100, current → target (enterprise-grade).

## Overall: ≈ 42 / 100 — "competent SMB boutique," not "safe for a major budget"

| Dimension | Score | One-line verdict |
|---|:--:|---|
| Enterprise trust & credibility | **38** | Honest & capable, but proofed/governed for SMB; fails the procurement/InfoSec gate |
| B2B conversion & messaging | **31** | Whole funnel screams SMB ("free audit / $600 credit / booked jobs"); enterprise buyer opts out fast |
| Design, layout, structure & UX | **64** | Good craft, but "agency-loud" SMB register, too long, all-mockup, no human faces |

**The single biggest finding (all three agents agree):** the site is built for a $4K/mo local-service
owner. To win big B2B clients it needs an **enterprise track** — different positioning, proof, trust
governance, conversion path, and a more restrained visual register.

---

## The strategic fork (decide this first — it sets scope)

**Option A — Dual audience:** keep the SMB local-service site AND add a dedicated **/enterprise** (or
/b2b) track: its own landing page, nav entry, messaging, proof, and "request a strategy session"
conversion path. Lower risk, keeps existing SMB lead-gen working, adds the enterprise surface on top.
*(Recommended — matches the real business while opening the enterprise door.)*

**Option B — Full reposition:** rebrand the entire site to target enterprise/B2B (new verticals like
SaaS / financial services / manufacturing, drop the local-service + Ontario framing, replace the
freebie funnel everywhere). Higher impact, higher risk, and it walks away from the current SMB pipeline.

Everything below is written so it works for **either** option — most items are universal trust/quality
upgrades; the items marked **[ENT]** are the ones that specifically create the enterprise track.
